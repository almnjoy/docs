Your AI assistant can send and receive WhatsApp messages. The Setup Assistant handles the full process — no CLI or technical access required.

## Connecting WhatsApp

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Setup Assistant">
    In your chat interface, switch to the **Setup Assistant** agent and say:

    > "Help me connect to WhatsApp"

    The Setup Assistant will automatically install the WhatsApp plugin and configure it for you.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan the QR Code">
    Once the plugin is installed, the Setup Assistant will generate a QR code and share a link like:

    `https://yourname.madeformeai.com/files/whatsapp-qr.png`

    Open that link in your browser, then:

    1. Open WhatsApp on your phone
    2. Go to **Settings → Linked Devices → Link a Device**
    3. Scan the QR code

    <Warning>Scan within 60 seconds — the QR expires quickly. If it times out, just say "new QR" and the assistant will generate a fresh one.</Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="You're connected">
    The Setup Assistant will confirm once linked:

    > WhatsApp is ready.

    Your AI can now send and receive WhatsApp messages on your behalf.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## About your WhatsApp number

WhatsApp connects using your existing account — the same way WhatsApp Web works. This means your AI uses **your own phone number**.

To message your AI, you'd be messaging yourself — which works, but feels a bit odd. For a more natural experience, consider linking a second number (Google Voice, a spare SIM, etc.) so your AI has its own dedicated contact.

---

## Troubleshooting

**QR timed out before I could scan** — Say "new QR" and the assistant will generate a fresh one immediately.

**Setup Assistant seems confused or stuck** — Switch to Claude Sonnet in the model selector. The WhatsApp setup flow works best with a more capable model.

**Linked the wrong number** — Ask the Setup Assistant to disconnect WhatsApp and start over with a different number.
